### Task 5: Signal Peak Analysis
**Question:**
"Explain what the resolution (σ) and the Full Width Half Maximum (FWHM) of a signal peak in a distribution are. Derive the relation, FWHM= 2√2 log 2 σ, for the resolution σ of a Gaussian distribution."

**Example Answer:**
The resolution (σ) is the standard deviation of the Gaussian distribution, indicating the spread of the data. The FWHM is the width of the distribution at half its maximum height. For a Gaussian distribution, FWHM is derived from the properties of the Gaussian function: \( \text{FWHM} = 2\sqrt{2 \ln 2} \cdot \sigma \approx 2.355 \cdot \sigma \).
