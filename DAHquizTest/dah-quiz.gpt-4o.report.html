### Question 5:
**Text:**
"A peak in a data distribution... Explain what the resolution (σ) and the Full Width Half Maximum (FWHM) of a signal peak in a distribution are. Derive the relation, FWHM= 2√2 log 2 σ..."

**Example Answer:**
Resolution (σ) is the standard deviation of the Gaussian distribution, indicating the spread of the data. FWHM is the width of the peak at half its maximum height. The relation is derived from the properties of a Gaussian function: \(FWHM = 2\sqrt{2 \ln 2} \cdot \sigma \approx 2.355 \cdot \sigma\).
